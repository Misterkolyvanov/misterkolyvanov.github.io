A Little Note

Hi there,

Thanks for stopping by! Before I share my work experience and recent projects with you,
I wanted to let you in on some things that are super important to me:


1. I want to make people’s lives BETTER


Apps can literally change how people live their lives, how they work, how they enjoy their free time, and how they manage their money. I've had my life completely changed by finding apps that uniquely answered some specific pain point or consistent struggle in my life (Google Sheets, Todoist, and YNAB are a few). I believe digital products will continue to transform our lives for the better and it makes me super happy that I get to contribute to that!




2. I like DIGGING into complex problems


There's nothing like learning the ins and outs of a problem, of looking at it from every angle, and of coming up with a solution that not only resolves the pain point, but takes the user's experience from just "fixed" to absolutely delightful. It takes a certain type of curiosity to not be afraid of the unknown; to jump into the abyss, to follow lines of thinking and questions through to their end. And then to finally gain perspective and deep, contextual understanding; to begin to see the larger systems at play that exist outside the realm of the initial problem.. Yeah, I like that stuff. :)




3. I love DESTROYING the boring, menial stuff people don't want to do at work anymore


I've worked in all kinds of different jobs and industries in the early part of my career (have held positions as a School Records Clerk, a Head Secretary, an Inventory Pricing Auditor, an Account Coordinator, among a few others), and after getting a grasp on the job, I would quickly get discouraged when I'd have to do some menial tasks over and over again on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis. I love helping people get efficient in the tedious areas of work so they can be free to focus on the meaningful and impactful things instead.



Anyways... Now let's get down to the nitty gritty...

Solutions included: an Administrative Leave Reporting Tool, a District Calendaring System for schools and HR, a district-wide State Reporting Time-Triggered Auto-Email Reminder System, a Student Safety Check In/Out System used throughout the district, a Project & Task Manager Tool, as well as Input Systems that gathered data district-wide for reporting and data mining purposes.

Obviously, a nerd's playground!

Internal Backlog Tool

Created a fully fleshed out product backlogging & requirements gathering process and Google Sheet tool that was then applied as a requirement for all projects company-wide.

A Product Backlog is a major communication tool that helps unify the expectations and requirements for both the Client / Stakeholders as well as the Product Team.

A good Product Backlog can be used to align vision, to negotiate / discuss priorities (what should we do first?), to capture ideas / new requirements, and to get an overall understanding of timeline and effort remaining.

Online Mortgage Application User Testing

Our client asked us to test the user experience between their product and several major products that were already on the market. We created a comprehensive testing plan that involved 3-hour long user testing sessions.

The test sessions were 3 hours long because we had the test subjects actually apply for a mortgage using both our client's product as well as for one of the competitor's products (we did not tell the test subject which product was our client's and we alternated which one the test subject completed first), which meant we had to find a very specific cohort of test subjects that were actually looking to buy a house, and we had to find unique ways of marketing and incentives to intrigue the ideal candidates.

After the testing was concluded, we compiled comprehensive reports that broke down the findings for each milestone of the mortgage application process, and listed out specific UX improvements that our client could implement to improve their product and make the experience better than any of their competitor's.
